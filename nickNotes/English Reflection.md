An Irish Airman Foresees His Death by W.B. Yeats explores the paradox of the airman's motivation, challenging human experiences. The airman is differentiated from the norm as he does not feel any motivation from most of the things that soldiers usually get motivated by. Yeats uses the repetition of the words "Those that I" followed by contradicting statements, allowing us to see that he doesn't fight out of hatred and he doesn't protect out of love.
